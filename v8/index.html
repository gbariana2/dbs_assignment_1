PREAMBLE

We are 50,000+ graduate students in Chicago. We sit in our silos. We attend our seminars. We eat lunch alone at our desks. We have been told that LinkedIn DMs are "networking." We have been told that awkward career fairs build community. We reject this.

This pamphlet is an act of rebellion against isolation in higher education. Read it. Share it. Act on it.

THE DECLARATIONS

seven principles for the abolition of graduate student loneliness

I.

WE BELIEVE THAT NETWORKING IS DEAD.

The word itself is a corpse. We do not "network." We meet humans. We drink coffee. We talk about research, about life, about the unbearable weight of a dissertation. This is not transactional. This is survival.

II.

NO MORE DISCIPLINARY PRISONS.

Your department is not your identity. A physicist should talk to a poet. A business student should argue with a theologian. The best ideas in history were born at the intersection. (Citation needed? We don't care.)

III.

RANDOMNESS IS A RADICAL ACT.

You will not choose your match. The algorithm will. You will sit across from a stranger and discover that the person you never would have emailed is the person you needed to meet. Strategic connections — serendipity.

IV.

ONE COFFEE. ONE HOUR. ZERO AGENDAS.

There is no pitch deck. There is no elevator speech. There is a cup of coffee and sixty minutes of unstructured, gloriously inefficient conversation. This is the point.

V.

CHICAGO IS THE CAMPUS.

Hyde Park to Evanston. The Loop to Lincoln Park. Rogers Park to Bronzeville. Seven universities. One city. The L train is our hallway. The coffeeshop is our seminar room.

VI.

WE REJECT PERFORMATIVE PROFESSIONALISM.

Come as you are. In your hoodie. With your imposter syndrome. With your half-formed thesis idea. With your existential dread about the job market. We are all figuring it out. Nobody has it together. Nobody.

VII.

THIS IS NOT AN APP. THIS IS A MOVEMENT.

We are not a startup. We are not disrupting anything. We are simply insisting that graduate students in the same city should know each other. That's it. That's the whole thing.

HOW THE MACHINE WORKS

the operational details, for the skeptics among you

01

YOU SIGN UP

Name. Email. School. Program. That's it. No headshot. No bio. No "describe yourself in three words." We already know you're tired.

02

WE MATCH YOU

Every two weeks, you receive an email with someone from a different program at a different university. The match is random. Gloriously, beautifully random.

03

YOU MEET

Pick a coffeeshop. Split the difference on geography. Sit down. Talk. Listen. Disagree. Laugh. Be a human being for one hour.

04

REPEAT

New match. New person. New perspective. The cycle continues. The network (we hate that word) grows organically.
