Campus Coffee Roulette

A quiet way for Chicago grad students to meet over coffee.

What this is

Once a week, we pair two graduate students from different programs at different universities across Chicago. You get a name, an email, and a suggestion: meet for coffee. That's the whole thing.

No app to download, no profile to maintain, no algorithm watching what you click. Just two people who wouldn't have met otherwise, sitting across from each other with something warm to drink.

How it works

Sign up

Share your name, university email, and program. Takes about a minute.

Get matched

Each Monday, you receive an email with your match — someone from a different program, often a different school.

Grab coffee

Find a time, pick a spot, show up. Some weeks it's twenty minutes. Some weeks you'll talk for two hours.

If a week doesn't work, just skip it. No penalty, no streak to protect. This fits your life — it doesn't compete with it.

Who this is for

Graduate school can be extraordinarily narrow. You spend years becoming the world's expert on one small thing, surrounded by people doing the same. It's wonderful and it's isolating.

This is for the student who wants to hear what someone in a completely different field thinks about over lunch. The urban planning student and the particle physicist. The divinity student and the computer scientist.
