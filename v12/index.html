Campus Coffee Roulette Launches Ambitious Plan
to Unite Chicago’s Graduate Students

Revolutionary matching program pairs scholars across seven universities for weekly coffee chats; organizers predict “an unprecedented era of cross-disciplinary collaboration”

By The Editorial Board  |  CHICAGO — March 26, 2026

A bold new initiative is poised to transform the landscape of graduate education across Chicago’s most prestigious institutions. Campus Coffee Roulette, a free matching service launching this spring, will pair graduate students from different universities and programs for informal coffee meetings—breaking down the institutional silos that have long defined academic life in the Windy City.

The program, which organizers describe as “academic speed-dating without the awkwardness,” uses a simple but effective algorithm to match students from different disciplines and universities each week. Participants sign up once, indicate their availability, and receive a weekly match via email with a suggested meeting spot.

“We realized that the greatest untapped resource in Chicago’s graduate ecosystem isn’t a new lab or library—it’s the brilliant person studying something completely different at the university across town,” said the program’s founders.

“The greatest untapped resource in Chicago’s graduate ecosystem is the brilliant person studying something completely different at the university across town.”

Initial pilot data from a small trial conducted last semester yielded extraordinary results. Of the 120 participants, 89 percent reported making at least one meaningful professional connection, while 34 percent initiated collaborative research projects with their coffee partners. Perhaps most remarkably, 97 percent said they would participate again.

The program deliberately eschews the hyper-optimized, algorithm-driven approach favored by commercial networking platforms. There are no profiles to curate, no endorsements to solicit, no connections to accumulate like digital trophies. Instead, Coffee Roulette returns to the oldest networking technology known to academia: two people, a table, and a warm beverage.

University administrators across the city have expressed enthusiastic support. Graduate deans at the University of Chicago, Northwestern, UIC, Loyola, DePaul, and IIT have all endorsed the program, with several offering to subsidize coffee purchases for participants through their student affairs offices.

Registration opens today. Graduate students at any of the seven participating institutions may enroll using the classified notice on this page. Matching begins the first week of April, with participants receiving their first pairing by electronic mail on Monday, April 6th.

How the Programme Works

Staff Report

I.
Register Once. Complete the brief classified form below with your name, university, and program of study. No photograph or biography required.
II.
Receive Your Match. Each Monday, you will receive by electronic mail the name and programme of a fellow graduate student at a different institution.
III.
Meet for Coffee. Arrange a 30–45 minute coffee meeting at a mutually convenient café. We suggest a location near the midpoint between your campuses.
IV.
Expand Your World. Discuss your research, share perspectives, discover unexpected common ground. Repeat weekly.
