Campus Coffee Roulette

Hello, friend.

Graduate school can feel vast and solitary — long hours in the library, the quiet hum of a lab at midnight, entire semesters passing inside one building. It doesn't have to be that way.

Campus Coffee Roulette is a simple idea: every two weeks, we pair you with another grad student from a different program somewhere across Chicago. You meet for coffee. That's it.

No agenda, no networking pressure, no icebreaker games. Just two people, two cups of coffee, and a conversation that wouldn't have happened otherwise.

How it works

You sign up with your name and university email. Every other Friday, you receive a short, quiet email introducing you to your match — their name, their program, and a few suggested coffee spots between your campuses.

You reach out. You find a time. You sit down together. The rest takes care of itself.

Who it's for

Graduate students at any of Chicago's universities — UChicago, Northwestern, UIC, Loyola, DePaul, Illinois Tech, and beyond. Master's students, PhD candidates, MBA cohorts, law students, medical residents. Anyone still learning.

We match across programs intentionally. A philosopher meets an engineer. A public health researcher sits down with someone studying poetry. The best conversations happen at the edges of disciplines.
